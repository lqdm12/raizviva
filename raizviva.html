You don't visit Raiz Viva. You live it — briefly, but deeply.

Raiz Viva is not a retreat center. It's a working farm inside the Atlantic Forest, where we grow food, build with earth, and learn daily from the forest.

We host small immersions — never more than eight people — so you work alongside us, not watch from outside.

This is not a performance of sustainability. It's slow, physical, sometimes messy work. That's the point.

Atlantic Forest

Only 12% remains. We are replanting indigenous species alongside food crops to rebuild corridors for jaguars, toucans, and thousands of endemic plants.

Agroforestry

No monoculture. We plant in layers — banana, cacao, inga, hardwood — so the forest feeds us while it grows.

Natural Building

Walls from the site: clay, sand, fiber. Foundations from reclaimed tires. Roofs that breathe. Built to last with minimal impact.

From the forest to the table

You'll eat things you can't find on any menu.

We cook almost entirely from what we grow. That means PANCs — Non-Conventional Edible Plants — wild greens, roots, and fruits that have fed this forest for generations.

Ora-pro-nóbis
High-protein climbing cactus, sautéed with garlic
Taioba
Large leaf, cooked like collards
Capuchinha
Peppery flowers and leaves
Beldroega
Succulent, lemony ground cover
Serralha
Wild bitter green, excellent raw
Caruru
Amaranth cousin, rich in iron

Where is it?

Santa Teresa region, Espírito Santo, 800m elevation in the Atlantic Forest. Remote, off-grid, full phone signal.

What do I need to bring?

Boots, headlamp, work clothes. We provide bedding, tools, and meals. Bring curiosity, we handle the rest.
